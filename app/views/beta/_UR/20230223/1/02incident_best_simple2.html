The content in these sections is exactly as the original reporter wrote it.

1. Why was the customer in contact with us?

Claimant attended his appointment to end existing sanctions on 1 December 2022 at 11:30am

2. How did the incident start? Was there a specific trigger?

He initially went to use the computers (WADS) at the Jobcentre however security came over and asked him to come off the computer. He was 2 hours late for this appointment and arrived at 13:30. I began to explain the process of ending the sanction. Claimant asked lots of questions regarding how much money he would lose, when he could get a hardship payment and also asked for a phone. I explained I would tell the claimant once I had worked out the answers about his pay and advised we wouldn't give him a phone since he had already had one from 22nd March 2022.

3. What were the issues with the customer's behaviour?

Claimant then made the "wanker" hand gesture. I told the claimant I could see what he was doing and if he continued to display inappropriate behaviour I would ask him to leave and not end the sanction. Claimant became disinterested and started to look around the room. He then put his hand down his shorts and clearly continued the hand gesture while holding his penis.

4. Tell us what happened next. How did you try to resolve the situation, and how did the customer react?

I saw this and said to the claimant "that's disgusting stop that". The claimant stopped, stood up, turned around and put his hand down the back of his shorts, pulling them down slightly as he did this to reveal his backside a little. I told the claimant to sit down and conduct himself appropriately. I explained that kind of behaviour was unacceptable and wouldn't be tolerated.

5. Did any of your colleagues see or hear what happened?

Yes

6. How did your interaction with the customer end?

I allowed the claimant to leave.

7. How did you feel during the incident?

I was initially upset by his behaviour but quickly recovered and got on with my day.
